01 — The Ruin

Nobody built this garden.
It simply refused to stay a ruin.

Behind an unmarked door in the old medina sits a house that gave up being a house a long time ago. The roof went first, then the walls softened, and the courtyard filled quietly with banana leaf, bougainvillea and a fig tree that now shades half the tables.

We didn't restore it. We fed it — a firepit where the reception room used to be, tagines lit at dawn where a family once slept, and glasses of orange juice sweating onto tiled tables that remember four centuries of rain.

02 — The Table

What comes out of the kitchen
depends on the morning market.

Everything is cooked to order in copper and clay, over charcoal, by cooks who learned these recipes at home before they learned them for guests. Nothing is plated to look still — it keeps cooking at your table.

Signature

Tagine of chicken, preserved lemon & olive

Slow‑braised with ginger, saffron and onion until the sauce turns to silk. Served the way it's always been served here — still bubbling, with bread for scooping.

Morning

Eggs baked in spiced tomato

Our version of the garden's breakfast bowl — merguez, cumin and fresh coriander, eaten under the fig tree with the first coffee.

From the coast

Sardines in chermoula, copper tagine

Rolled around a garlic‑herb chermoula and finished in tomato with sweet pepper. A short drive from the Atlantic, a long way from ordinary.

Lighter

Seared salmon, lemon couscous

Crisp‑skinned and set over couscous tossed with cucumber, tomato and mint — the one dish on the menu that argues for a siesta after.

For the table

Lamb shank, roasted roots

Cooked until the bone slides free on its own, surrounded by turnip, carrot and courgette that spent the afternoon in the same pot.

Sweet & savoury

Tagine of prune, apricot & almond

The classic Fassi pairing of meat and fruit, finished with toasted almonds — sweetness used the way this city has always used it, sparingly and on purpose.

To finish

Pastilla, orange & cinnamon

Warm filo, a soft vegetable filling, cinnamon traced in lines across icing sugar. Cut it while it's still crackling.

To share

Trio of garden salads

Smoked aubergine zaalouk, potato with herbs, and sweet pepper — the quiet dishes that make everything else taste better.
